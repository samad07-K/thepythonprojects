India on Sunday strongly rejected Pakistan’s allegations that it was involved in a recent terrorist attack in Karachi. The Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) called the claims unfounded and advised Pakistan to focus on addressing terrorism within its own borders.
Responding to media queries, MEA spokesperson Randhir Jaiswal stated that instead of blaming others, Pakistan should focus on addressing its internal issues and take credible action against the terror infrastructure operating from its own territory. He added that Pakistan must stop relying on terrorism as an instrument of state policy and work towards dismantling such networks within its borders.
We have seen Pakistani reports making baseless allegations against India regarding the recent incident in Karachi. We categorically reject them. Instead of pointing fingers at others, Pakistan would do better to look inwards, take credible action against the terror infrastructure on its territory and rid itself of its proclivity to rely on terrorism as an instrument of state policy,” Jaiswal said.
